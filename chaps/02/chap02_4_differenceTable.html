    \[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} f(0.1) = & f_0 + s\Delta f_0 + \frac{s(s-1)}{2!}\Delta^2 f_0 + \frac{s(s-1)(s-2)}{3!}\Delta^3 f_0 \\ & + \frac{s(s-1)(s-2)(s-3)}{4!}\Delta^4 f_0 \\ = & 1+0.1(1)+\frac{0.1(0.1-1)}{2}(1) + \frac{0.1(0.1-1)(0.1-2)}{6}(1) \\ & + \frac{0.1(0.1-1)(0.1-2)(0.1-3)}{24}(0.1) \\ = & 1+0.1 - 0.045 +0.0285 -0.0206625 \\ = & 1.0628375 \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Solution

  • Choose \(x_j=x_0=0.5\);

  • Calculate \(\displaystyle s=\frac{x-x_0}{h}=\frac{0.55-0.5}{0.2}=0.25\);

  • Using \(\displaystyle f_s = f_0 + s\Delta f_0 + \frac{s(s-1)}{2!}\Delta^2 f_0 + \frac{s(s-1)(s-2)}{3!}\Delta^3 f_0 + \frac{s(s-1)(s-2)(s-3)}{4!}\Delta^4 f_0 +\cdots\)

  • We can keep up to 4-th order difference for this question:

\[\begin{split} \begin{aligned} f(0.55) = P_4(0.55) = & 0.47943 + 0.25\times 0.16479 + \frac{0.25\times(-0.75)}{2}\times(-0.02568) \\ & + \frac{0.25\times(-0.75)\times(-1.75)}{6}\times(-0.00555) \\ & + \frac{0.25\times(-0.75)\times(-1.75)\times(-2.75)}{24}\times(0.00124) \\ = & 0.47943 + 0.04120 + 0.00241 - 0.00030 - 0.00005\\ = & 0.52268 = 0.5227~~\text{to 4 decimal places.} \end{aligned} \end{split}\]

Note

The tabulated function is in fact \(\, f(x)=\sin x\,\) and note that \(\,\sin(0.55) = 0.5227\,\) to \(4\) decimal places.
